Why Pairing?

Introduction...

Pair programming is an increasingly popular technique wherein two programmers work together on one workstation. The Driver actively writes code while the Navigator directs the Driver and/or reviews the code. Pair programming is part of the Agile Software Development strategy (Agile what?) and is becoming a standard method of software development.

Two heads are better than one...

  • According to Wikipedia, pairs spend 15% more time on programs than individuals with 15% fewer defects in the final programs (More study reviews). Catching errors while they happen not only saves time but also saves money.
  • Pairs can learn strategies and techniques from each other. Each person brings different skills and knowledge to the team.
  • Better strategies are developed with immediate peer review.
  • It is more enjoyable than working alone and increases confidence in the quality of the final product.
  • If pair programming occurs with other members of the development team it helps to build communication skills and camaraderie. Also, all members of the team feel more included in the project and feel a greater amount of responsibility for the project's success.

Steps for success...

  1. Introduction and check-in
  2. Work approach check-in
  3. Appoint a driver and navigator and be sure to switch roles occasionally
  4. Discuss the goals/assignment for the pairing session
  5. Screenshare with Stypi or Collabedit or other live code-sharing program
  6. Research methods, check your partner's work, share opinions and ask lots of questions
  7. Above all have fun and communicate!

Last but not least...

REFLECT! No, not what your mirror does but what every pair should do at the end of a session. It helps to clarify and solidify new concepts. This webpage was the product of a paired programming session.
