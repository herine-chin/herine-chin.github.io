Teach your dog to sit

Introduction...

Teaching your pup to sit is one of the most important tricks you could ever teach. It's part obedience training and part bonding. Wouldn't it be easier to put a collar on your furbaby while they are sitting rather than walking around? How about sitting when a child wants to pet them?

What you'll need...

  • Clicker (pictured)
  • Treats (Yummy and bite-sized)
  • Time (15-30 minutes)
  • Space (5'x5' minimum)
Caption: Clickers come in all kinds of designs.

Philosophy...

Approach each pup with love and they will love you back for it. When teaching a trick, I like to use positive reinforcement. You are basically rewarding your pup when they do something right.

Clicker technique...

Before we teach the actual trick, we have to acclimate your puppy to the clicker. A pup can be confused with the multiple verbal praises given during training. The clicker provides a clear signal that something was done right so the pup can start associating their behavior to what you want.

  1. Click your clicker.
  2. If your pup looks at you, immediately reward them with a treat. If they do not, repeat step 1 until they do.

The tricky part...(not really)

  1. Stand in front of your pup with treat and clicker in hand.
  2. Hold the treat in front of your pup's nose and slightly above their head. The pup will naturally lean back and sit. Adjust your hand if necessary.
  3. Say "Sit" when they do.
  4. Click and reward with a treat!
  5. Repeat 5-10 times
  6. Practice daily and slowly move your further away with each session. Eventually your pup will sit on command instead of following your hand.

CONGRATS! You did it.
